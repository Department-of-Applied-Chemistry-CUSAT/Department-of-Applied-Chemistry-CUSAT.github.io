Faculty Profiles

Prof. K. Girish Kumar
giri@cusat.ac.in
Professor
Analytical chemistry

Dr. K Girish Kumar received his Bachelor and Post Graduate degree in Chemistry from University of Kerala in 1982 and 1984 respectively. He obtained his Ph.D in Analytical Chemistry from University of Kerala in1989. He started his career in 1989 as Lecturer in the Dept. of Chemistry, Gandhigram Rural Institute Tamil Nadu and in 1998 became Reader in Chemistry. In 1999 he joined the Dept. of Applied Chemistry, Cochin University of Science and Technology as Reader in Analytical Chemistry. In 2006 he became Professor of Analytical Chemistry and has served as Head of the Department of Applied Chemistry during 2007-2010 (rotation). He has been a Visiting Professor at the Dept. of Chemistry, Sultan Qaboos University, Muscat, Sultanate of Oman. He has research collaboration with Dept. of Biosensors in Food, Institute of Animal Reproduction and Food Research, POLAND. Having supervised 15 Ph.Ds he has been conferred with the One-Time Research Grant of the UGC in 2014. He has 26 years of teaching experience and has been actively involved in research for 29 years.

The book authored by Dr. K. Girish Kumar “Plastic Kathayum Karyavum” published by NBS, Kerala depicts the story of plastic for the layman. Not restricting his activities to the scientific community, Dr. K. Girish Kumar has also published articles for the layman on various environmental and health aspects of chemicals used in daily life. His research interests include Development of optical/fluorescent sensors, Fabrication of electrochemical sensors for determination of metal ions, pharmaceuticals, biologically active molecules and food additives, Spectrophotometric methods for pharmaceuticals, Trace analysis of metals, Ion removal studies, Electrode modification using carbon nanotubes, conducting polymers, metal nanoparticles and self- assembled monolayers and Fabrication of Biosensors. He has over 78 research publications in Indian and international journals. He has undertaken many research projects from various agencies like UGC, CSIR, DRDO, KSCSTE and DST.

Dr. Kala R

Dr. Kala R received her M.Sc degree in Chemistry from University of Kerala in 1999. Dr. Manoj E
manoje@cusat.ac.in
Assistant Professor
Coordination Chemistry

We focus on designing new multinuclear transition metal complexes of polydentate Schiff base ligands by applying the knowledge base in coordination chemistry. Spectral and structural studies are our prime area of investigations. The structural modulations of the metal complexes are based on their probable applications mainly on biological implications, photovoltaics and magnetic properties. Self-assembled metallosupramolecular systems based on novel building blocks are another area of research emphasis. The nanoscopic self-assembled systems are considered as potential materials for wide applications.

Selected Publications
1. Molecular trail for the anticancer behavior of a novel copper carbohydrazone complex in BRCA1 mutated breast cancer. R.S. Nair, E. Manoj, R. Thankappan, S.K. Chandrika, M.R.P. Kurup, P. Srinivas, Mol Carcinog. 56 (2017) 1501.

2. A metal complex and a process thereof, P. Srinivas, M.R.P. Kurup, E. Manoj, R.S. Nair, Indian Pat. No. 286230 dt 10-08-2017.

3. Self-assembled macrocyclic molecular squares of Ni(II) derived from carbohydrazones and thiocarbohydrazones: Structural and magnetic studies, E. Manoj, M.R.P. Kurup, H.-K. Fun, A. Punnoose, Polyhedron, 26 (2007) 4451.

4. Macrocyclic molecular square complex of zinc(II) self-assembled with a carbohydrazone ligand, E. Manoj, M.R.P. Kurup, H.-K. Fun, Inorganic Chemistry Communications, 10 (2007) 324.

Dr. N. Manoj
manoj.n@cusat.ac.in
Professor
Organic Chemistry

Dr. N. Manoj received M.Sc. degree in Organic Chemistry from the School of Chemical Sciences, Mahatma Gandhi University, Kottayam, India in 1993. After M. Sc., he joined the research group of Dr. K. R. Gopidas, Photochemistry Research Unit, Regional Research Laboratory-CSIR (Now NIIST-CSIR), Trivandrum, India, with the prestigious Dr. K. S. Krishnan Junior Research Fellowship and obtained his Ph.D. degree (1999) in Chemistry from Mahatma Gandhi University, Kottayam, India. He worked as a post – doctoral research associate at the Department of Chemistry at the Politechnic University of Valencia, Spain (2000-2001) and thereafter at the Lehrstuhl für Umweltmesstechnik, Engler-Bunte Institut, Universität Karlsruhe, Germany (2002-2006). He has undertaken teaching assignments in physical chemistry to MS students as guest faculty at the University of Applied Sciences, Karlsruhe during the years 2002-2003. Dr. Manoj has joined this Department as a Reader in Organic Chemistry in 2006, became Associate Professor in 2009 since then he teaches Organic Chemistry and leads a research group in Organic Chemistry and Photochemistry. Currently he is the Head of the Department of Department of Applied Chemistry, member of the Board of Studies in Applied Chemistry and a member of the Academic Council of the Cochin University of Science and Technology. He is a life member of Chemical Research Society of India, Association of Chemistry Teachers and Swadeshi Science Movement. His research interests include Photochemistry, Supramolecular Chemistry, Photoinduced electron Transfer, Dye sensitized solar cells and Chemistry of reactive oxygen species.

Dr. P. M. Sabura Begum
pmsabura@cusat.ac.in
Associate Professor
Organic Chemistry

Dr. Sabura Begum received her B.Sc. degree (1988) from Holy Cross College, Tiruchirappalli and M.Sc. degree (1990) from M.E.S. College, Kalady, University of Calicut. She pursued her M.Phil (1991) and Ph. D (2010) with Dr. K.K. Mohammed Yusuff and Dr. Rani Joseph from Cochin University of Science and Technology. Dr. Sindhu M
sindhumathai@cusat.ac.in
Assistant Professor
Organic Chemistry

Dr. Sindhu Mathai received her Ph. D. from Cochin University of Science and Technology in February 2005, and the research work was carried out at CSIR-NIIST, Trivandrum, under the guidance of Dr. G. Vijay Nair. During her doctoral studies, she worked on ‘Novel 1,3-dipolar cycloaddition reactions of acyclic carbonyl ylides and related chemistry’. Subsequently, she joined as a post-doctoral fellow (2005-2006) under the direction of Prof. Alan R. Katritzky, University of Florida, to investigate the synthesis of triazole polymers as potential rocket propellant binders. Afterward, she received the Alexander von Humboldt Fellowship to pursue her research (2006-2008) at Regensburg University, Germany, under the guidance of Prof. Dr. Oliver Reiser. While working as an Alexander von Humboldt Fellow, she was engaged in the multi-step total synthesis of biologically active natural products like Chinensiolides; members of the Guaianolide family. She returned to India in May 2008 and joined Lupin Research Park, Pune, as a Manager Grade Research Scientist in the Medicinal Chemistry Division and continued there till March 2010. There she focused on the investigation of antidiabetic agents and has one international patent to her credit in this regard. In the year 2012, Dr. Sindhu Mathai joined Catholicate College, Pathanamthitta, Kerala, as Assistant Professor of Chemistry and continued there until July 2019. During her service in the College, she completed one minor research project sponsored by UGC on ‘Investigations towards the synthetic applications of Gallium (III)triflate as a superior Lewis acid catalyst in organic transformation.’ Presently, she is working as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Applied Chemistry, Cochin University of Science and Technology. She has received the UGC-Start up grant in 2020 for the research project on ‘Investigations on the dipolar cycloaddition reactions of azomethine imines with 1,2-diones, 1,4-diones and bisarynes: synthesis of novel structurally important heterocycles’. She is a member of the P. G. board of studies, chemistry, M. G. University. Her current research interests include the development of novel methodologies in organic synthesis, heterogeneous catalysis, synthesis of bioactive molecules with special emphasis on antiviral drugs, and development of polymers for organic solar cells.

Prof. K. Sreekumar
ksk@cusat.ac.in
Professor
Catalysis

Dr. Sreekumar received his Ph.D from Mahatma Gandhi University, Kottayam, Kerala in 1989 in the area of Polymer Chemistry. He had worked as Lecturer and Reader in the Department of Chemistry, Kerala University from1989 to 1999. In 1999, he moved to Cochin University of Science and Technology to take up the assignment as Reader in Catalysis at the Department of Applied Chemistry, CUSAT. He has been a visiting scientist at the HEDM laboratory, NUS and EMRC, Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. He was a member of the Senate and Academic Council of CUSAT and represents the Board of Studies of several Universities and Institutes. He is a member of the Society for Polymer Science, India and High Energy Materials Society of India.

His major fields of research include:
• Polymer supported reagents and catalysts
• Dendrimer based organocatalysts
• Modified clay supported transition metal complexes as heterogeneous catalysts
• Solid phase organic synthesis – peptide synthesis
• Photochemistry and Photophysics of synthetic polymers
• Polymers for holographic applications - Data storage
• Polymers for optical lithography –Micro and Stereo lithography
• Polymers for optoelectronics and photonics – photovoltaic devices
• Conjugated Functional polymers - Optical limiting studies
• Chemosensors - Amplified quenching of fluorescence
• Polymeric NLO materials- Computational studies.
• Quantum Chemical calculations – DFT and Ab initio calculations.
• High energy density materials–polyphosphazenes, polyoxetanes and polyoxeranes as energetic binders
